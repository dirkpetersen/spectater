## Overview

All contractors, vendors, and partners engaged by the University must maintain insurance coverage as specified in this policy. These requirements protect the University and its employees, students, and assets from liability exposure during contractual relationships.

This policy applies to all work performed on University premises or involving University operations. Contractors must provide proof of insurance via a current Certificate of Insurance (ACORD form 25-S(06/14)) prior to commencement of work.

---

## Definitions

- **Certificate of Insurance (COI)**: Standard ACORD form 25-S(06/14) documenting active insurance coverage and named additional insureds
- **Certificate Holder**: The entity (contractor, vendor, partner) whose insurance is documented
- **Additional Insured**: Party added to an insurance policy to receive coverage protection
- **Primary and Non-Contributory**: Insurance listed is primary coverage that does not require other insurance to pay first
- **Per Occurrence Limit**: Maximum amount the insurer will pay for a single incident or claim
- **General Aggregate Limit**: Maximum amount the insurer will pay for all claims combined during the policy period
- **AM Best Rating**: Standard & Poor's insurance company financial strength rating (A- VII = Seventh highest financial strength category)
- **Waiver of Subrogation**: Agreement that insurance company will not sue the named party to recover claim amounts
- **Workers' Compensation**: Insurance covering employee medical expenses and lost wages due to work-related injury

## Insurance Requirements

### 1. Certificate Holder Information

#### 1.1 Certificate Holder Name
- **Requirement**: The Certificate Holder name on the COI must exactly match the legal entity name provided in the contract or application
- **Examples of PASS**:
  - Contract shows: "ACME Contracting, LLC" | COI shows: "ACME Contracting, LLC" ✅
  - Contract shows: "ACME Contracting, Inc." | COI shows: "ACME Contracting, Inc." ✅
- **Examples of FAIL**:
  - Contract shows: "ACME Contracting, LLC" | COI shows: "ACME Contracting" ❌ (Missing LLC designation)
  - Contract shows: "ACME Contracting, LLC" | COI shows: "ACME Contracting, Inc." ❌ (Wrong entity type)
- **Why**: Ensures insurance is issued for the correct contracting entity

#### 1.2 Certificate Holder Address
- **Requirement**: Certificate Holder address must match the primary business address provided in the contract or application
- **Examples of PASS**:
  - Application provides: "123 Main St, Portland, OR 97201" | COI shows: "123 Main St, Portland, OR 97201" ✅
  - Application provides: "123 Main St, Suite 100, Portland, OR 97201" | COI shows: "123 Main St, Suite 100, Portland, OR 97201" ✅
- **Examples of FAIL**:
  - Application provides: "123 Main St, Portland, OR" | COI shows: "123 Main St, Beaverton, OR" ❌ (Different city)
  - Application provides: "123 Main St, Portland, OR" | COI shows: "456 Oak Ave, Portland, OR" ❌ (Different address)
- **Why**: Confirms the Certificate of Insurance is for the correct business location

### 3. General Liability Coverage

#### 3.1 General Liability Coverage Requirement
- **Requirement**: Certificate must include **General Liability (Commercial General Liability / CGL)** coverage
- **Requirement**: Coverage must be active and not expired as of the start date of contract work
- **Examples of PASS**:
  - "General Liability - Per Occurrence Limit: $2,000,000" ✅
  - "Commercial General Liability - Coverage effective through [future date]" ✅
- **Examples of FAIL**:
  - General Liability line is blank or not listed ❌
  - General Liability coverage is expired ❌
- **Why**: General Liability is the primary insurance protecting against bodily injury and property damage claims

#### 3.2 General Liability - Per Occurrence Limit
- **Requirement**: General Liability per occurrence limit must be **minimum $[TO BE FILLED: amount]** (suggest $2,000,000)
- **Examples of PASS**:
  - "GL Per Occurrence: $2,000,000" ✅ Meets requirement
  - "GL Per Occurrence: $5,000,000" ✅ Exceeds requirement
- **Examples of FAIL**:
  - "GL Per Occurrence: $1,000,000" ❌ Below minimum
  - "GL Per Occurrence: Not stated" ❌ Missing information
- **Why**: Establishes minimum protection level per incident

#### 3.3 General Liability - General Aggregate Limit
- **Requirement**: General Liability General Aggregate limit must be **minimum $[TO BE FILLED: amount]** (suggest $2,000,000)
- **Requirement**: General Aggregate must be at least equal to the per occurrence limit
- **Examples of PASS**:
  - "GL General Aggregate: $2,000,000" ✅
  - "GL General Aggregate: $4,000,000" ✅ (Higher than per occurrence is OK)
- **Examples of FAIL**:
  - "GL General Aggregate: $1,000,000" ❌ Below minimum
  - "GL General Aggregate: Blank/Missing" ❌
- **Why**: Establishes total annual protection limit

#### 3.4 Damage to Rented Premises Endorsement
- **Requirement**: General Liability must include **Damage to Rented Premises** endorsement
- **Requirement**: Damage to Rented Premises limit must be **minimum $[TO BE FILLED: amount]** (suggest $300,000 or 50% of per occurrence limit)
- **Examples of PASS**:
  - "Damage to Rented Premises - Limit: $300,000" ✅
  - "DRPE included - Limit: $500,000" ✅
- **Examples of FAIL**:
  - "Damage to Rented Premises: Not Included" ❌
  - "Damage to Rented Premises limit: $100,000" ❌ (Too low)
  - "GL coverage present but DRPE not listed" ❌
- **Why**: Ensures the University is protected if contractor's work causes damage to University facilities or rented spaces

  - "Additional Insured applies only to Workers' Comp, not GL" ❌

#### 4.2 Primary and Non-Contributory
- **Requirement**: Additional Insured coverage must be **Primary and Non-Contributory** to the University's own insurance
- **Requirement**: This means contractor's insurance pays claims first, before OSU's insurance is accessed
- **Examples of PASS**:
  - "Primary and Non-Contributory" notation shown on endorsement ✅
  - Certificate clearly states contractor's insurance is primary ✅
- **Examples of FAIL**:
  - "Secondary or Contributing" status shown ❌
  - No statement of primary/non-contributory status ❌
- **Why**: Ensures the University doesn't have to use its own insurance to cover contractor's liability

#### 8.2 Workers' Compensation - Employees vs. Independent Contractors
- **Requirement**: If contractor has **zero employees** and provides only independent work, Workers' Compensation may be **not applicable**
- **Requirement**: Contractor must provide documentation confirming independent contractor status if claiming exemption
- **Examples of PASS** (No employees):
  - "Workers' Comp: Not applicable - sole proprietor with no employees" ✅
  - Certificate shows "No Employees" field marked, and no WC coverage ✅
- **Examples of FAIL** (Has employees):
  - Certificate shows 5 employees but no Workers' Comp coverage ❌
  - Contractor claims to have staff but Workers' Comp not shown ❌
- **Examples of PASS** (Not applicable):
  - "Independent contractor status confirmed; no employee coverage required" ✅
  - Form 1099 contractor with no staff ✅
- **Why**: Workers' Compensation protects employees injured on the job and ensures OSU compliance with labor laws
